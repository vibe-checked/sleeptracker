📖 Features Guide

SleepTracker offers a comprehensive suite of sleep analysis tools. Here's what each feature does:

Sleep Rings
Three animated rings showing progress toward your sleep duration, quality, and deep sleep goals.
Sleep Stages
Detailed hypnogram showing your Awake, REM, Light, and Deep sleep phases throughout the night.
Readiness Score
A composite score reflecting your physical and mental readiness based on sleep quality and stress.
Sleep Fuel
Measures the quality and efficiency of your sleep beyond simple duration tracking.
Health Metrics
Heart rate, SpO2, HRV, respiration rate, and wrist temperature monitoring during sleep.
Sleep Bank
Track your cumulative sleep debt or surplus over the past week against your goals.
Smart Alarm
Wakes you during light sleep within your chosen window for a more refreshed morning.
Sleep Consistency
Visualize your bedtime patterns and get personalized bedtime recommendations.
Data Export
Export all your sleep data as a CSV file to share with healthcare providers.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How does SleepTracker detect sleep automatically?
SleepTracker uses advanced heuristics combining data from your Apple Watch's accelerometer, heart rate sensor, and other biometric inputs to automatically determine when you fall asleep and wake up. No manual input is required — just wear your Apple Watch to bed.
Do I need an Apple Watch to use SleepTracker?
An Apple Watch is recommended for the best experience, as it provides the biometric data used for sleep stage analysis and health metrics. However, if you don't wear your Watch to bed, the app can still track basic time-in-bed data.
How is my Sleep Rating calculated?
Your Sleep Rating is a composite score (0–100) based on sleep efficiency, deep sleep duration relative to your total sleep time, and overall sleep quality. Higher ratings indicate more restorative sleep.
What is Readiness and how is it different from Sleep Rating?
While Sleep Rating evaluates last night's sleep quality, Readiness is a forward-looking score that factors in your sleep quality, prior day stress, and recovery to indicate how prepared you are for the day ahead.
What does "Sleep Fuel" measure?
Sleep Fuel measures the restorative quality of your sleep by weighting deep sleep and REM sleep proportions alongside overall efficiency. It tells you how much "fuel" your body got from the night's rest, regardless of total time in bed.
How does the Sleep Bank work?
The Sleep Bank tracks your cumulative sleep balance over the past 7 days. Each night, the difference between your actual sleep and your goal (default: 8 hours) is added to your balance. A positive balance means you have surplus sleep; negative means you have sleep debt to recover.
How does the Smart Alarm know when to wake me?
The Smart Alarm monitors your sleep stages within the window you set (15, 30, or 45 minutes before your target wake time). It triggers the alarm when it detects you're in a lighter sleep phase, resulting in a more natural, less groggy awakening.
Is my health data shared with anyone?
Absolutely not. All data stays on your device. We don't use any analytics, advertising, or third-party services. See our Privacy Policy for full details.
How do I export my sleep data?
Go to Settings and tap "Export Sleep Data (CSV)." This downloads a CSV file containing 14 days of comprehensive sleep data including all health metrics, which you can open in any spreadsheet application or share with your doctor.
Can I change the app's appearance?
Yes! SleepTracker includes three themes: Midnight (deep blue, default), Neon (vibrant pink and gold), and Sunrise (warm amber and purple). Switch between them in Settings.

🔧 Troubleshooting

Sleep not being tracked

Make sure your Apple Watch is worn to bed and has sufficient battery. Check that SleepTracker has permission to access HealthKit data in Settings > Health > Data Access. If issues persist, try restarting both your iPhone and Apple Watch.

Inaccurate sleep detection

Sleep detection accuracy improves over time as the app learns your patterns. If you're a restless sleeper, you may need to adjust the sensitivity in the app's calibration settings. Allow 3–5 nights for the algorithm to adapt to your sleep style.

Missing health metrics

Blood oxygen (SpO2) requires Apple Watch Series 6 or later. Wrist temperature requires Apple Watch Series 8 or Ultra. Make sure these measurements are enabled in the Apple Watch Health settings.

App not showing on Apple Watch

Open the Watch app on your iPhone, scroll down to find SleepTracker, and ensure "Show on Apple Watch" is enabled. If it doesn't appear, try reinstalling the app.

⚙️ System Requirements

  • iPhone running iOS 16.0 or later
  • Apple Watch running watchOS 9.0 or later (recommended)
  • Apple Watch Series 6+ for blood oxygen monitoring
  • Apple Watch Series 8+ or Ultra for wrist temperature
